1

Park ₹1–2L in a liquid fund first

Before deploying ₹10L into markets, keep 3–6 months of expenses in a liquid mutual fund (e.g. HDFC Liquid Fund). This is your safety net — never invest your emergency fund in equity.

Non-negotiable first step
2

Don't invest ₹10L all at once — use STP

A Systematic Transfer Plan (STP) lets you park the lump sum in a liquid fund and auto-transfer a fixed amount monthly into equity. This protects you from investing at a market peak. Transfer ₹50,000–₹1L per month over 10–20 months.

Reduces timing risk
3

Allocate based on your goal timeline

Under 3 years: keep mostly in debt funds / FD. 3–7 years: 60% equity (Nifty 50 index) + 40% debt. Over 7 years: 70–80% equity index funds + 10–20% debt + 10% gold ETF.

Timeline drives allocation
4

Max out PPF (₹1.5L/yr) for guaranteed tax-free returns

PPF gives 7.1% fully tax-free + Section 80C deduction. For someone in the 30% tax bracket, the effective post-tax return is closer to 10%+. Contribute ₹1.5L annually as your "safe debt" allocation.

Tax-efficient
5

Add NPS if you want extra 80CCD(1B) deduction

NPS gives an additional ₹50,000 deduction beyond the ₹1.5L 80C limit. At 30% tax slab, that's ₹15,000 saved in tax instantly. The downside: money is locked till age 60 and 40% must be annuitised on exit.

For salaried, high-tax bracket
6

Rebalance annually — not more often

Once a year, check if your equity/debt split has drifted from your target. If equity has grown to 85% from your 70% target, sell some equity and buy debt to rebalance. This forces you to sell high and buy low automatically.

Annual discipline
✗

"I should invest ₹10L in one shot to get maximum returns"

Lump sum at a market peak can take 3–5 years just to break even. The 2008 crash saw Nifty drop 60% — a ₹10L lump sum became ₹4L in months. Using a Systematic Transfer Plan (STP) over 12–18 months removes this timing risk while your money still earns 6–7% sitting in a liquid fund.
✗

"With ₹10 Lakhs I should pick individual stocks for better returns"

Studies show over 90% of retail investors underperform the index over 10 years. Stock picking requires deep research, emotional discipline, and time. A Nifty 50 index fund gives you all 50 top Indian companies at 0.1% expense ratio — better than most active fund managers and far better than typical retail stock picks.
✗

"Real estate is the best investment — I should use ₹10L as a down payment"

₹10L as a home down payment typically means taking a ₹40–60L loan at 8.5% interest. Over 20 years you'll pay ₹40–70L in interest alone. Rental yields in India are only 2–3%. Equity index funds have historically outperformed real estate in most Indian cities over 10+ year horizons.
✗

"I need a financial advisor to manage ₹10 Lakhs"

For a simple portfolio (index fund + PPF + liquid fund), you don't need a paid advisor. Platforms like Groww, Zerodha Coin, and Paytm Money let you invest in direct plans with zero commission. If you want advice, only use SEBI-registered fee-only advisors — not commission-based agents who earn by selling you expensive regular plans.
✓

"Diversification across asset classes truly reduces risk"

Absolutely true. A portfolio of 70% equity + 20% debt + 10% gold historically delivers 80–90% of pure equity returns with significantly lower volatility. When equity crashes, gold and debt often hold or rise — cushioning the blow. Rebalancing annually between these three is one of the simplest, most powerful strategies for long-term wealth.
Jargon Buster

Finance terms,
in plain language

Searchable glossary of terms every Indian investor hears but nobody explains clearly.

🔍
XIRRReturns
Extended Internal Rate of Return. Measures the actual annualised return on your SIP investments, accounting for the fact that each instalment is invested on a different date. Use XIRR (not CAGR) to compare your mutual fund performance.
Expense RatioMutual Funds
Annual fee charged by a mutual fund as a % of your investment. Lower is better — Nifty 50 index funds charge ~0.1% vs 1–2% for active funds. On ₹10L over 20 years, that 1% difference compiles into lakhs of lost returns.
P/E RatioStocks
Price-to-Earnings Ratio. How much investors pay for ₹1 of a company's annual profit. Nifty 50 P/E averages 20–22. Above 25 = expensive market; below 18 = good time to invest more. A useful but imperfect market valuation signal.
LTCGTax
Long Term Capital Gains. Profit from equity held for more than 1 year. Taxed at 12.5% on gains above ₹1.25 Lakh annually (as of FY 2024–25). For debt funds, LTCG applies after 3 years and is taxed as per your income slab.
NAVMutual Funds
Net Asset Value. The per-unit price of a mutual fund, calculated daily. Like a stock price — you buy units at today's NAV and sell at a future NAV. A higher NAV doesn't mean expensive; it just means the fund has been around longer.
SIPInvesting
Systematic Investment Plan. Investing a fixed amount into a mutual fund every month, automatically. SIP builds discipline, averages your purchase cost over time (rupee cost averaging), and removes the temptation to time the market.
ELSSTax Saving
Equity Linked Savings Scheme. A mutual fund that invests in equities AND gives Section 80C tax deduction up to ₹1.5L/year. Lock-in period is only 3 years — the shortest among 80C options. Good for investors comfortable with equity risk.
Debt FundMutual Funds
A mutual fund that invests in bonds, government securities, and money market instruments. Lower risk than equity, returns of 6–8%, and higher liquidity than FDs. Good for short-term goals (1–3 years) or as the "stable" portion of your portfolio.
LumpsumInvesting
Investing a large amount all at once, rather than in instalments (SIP). Lumpsum works well when markets are at a low. For large amounts at uncertain market levels, use STP (Systematic Transfer Plan) to invest gradually over 12–18 months.
Rupee Cost AveragingStrategy
When you invest via SIP, you buy more units when the price is low and fewer when it's high — automatically averaging your cost over time. This is the core advantage of SIP over lumpsum investing in volatile markets.
Asset AllocationStrategy
How you divide your investments across different asset classes — typically Equity (stocks/mutual funds), Debt (bonds/FD/PPF), and Gold. A common starting rule: (100 – your age)% in equity, the rest in debt + gold.
RebalancingStrategy
Periodically adjusting your portfolio back to your target asset allocation. If equity grows from 70% to 80% of your portfolio, you sell some equity and buy debt to get back to 70%. This naturally forces you to sell high and buy low.

Checklist

The first-time investor
checklist

Complete these steps before investing your first rupee. Tick each one off as you go.

0 of 8 completed
✓
Get a PAN Card
Mandatory for all investments above ₹50,000 and for opening a Demat account. Apply online at incometax.gov.in — takes 10–15 minutes.
✓
Link Aadhaar to PAN
Required for KYC verification when opening investment accounts. Do it online at the Income Tax portal to avoid account deactivation.
✓
Open a savings account with good UPI support
You'll need this to transfer money to your investment accounts. Choose a bank with good mobile banking — HDFC, ICICI, Kotak, or SBI are popular choices.
✓
Build your emergency fund first
Park 3–6 months of expenses in a liquid mutual fund or high-yield savings account before investing in anything else. This is your safety net — never touch it for investments.
✓
Buy adequate term insurance
If anyone depends on your income, buy a pure term insurance plan first. Cover should be 10–15x your annual income. Don't mix insurance with investment (avoid ULIPs).
✓
Complete KYC for mutual funds
One-time KYC registration required for all mutual fund investments. Do it online via CVL KRA or through your chosen platform (Groww, Zerodha Coin, etc.).
✓
Open a Demat + Trading account (for stocks/ETFs)
Needed if you want to buy individual stocks or ETFs. Choose a discount broker — Zerodha, Groww, or Upstox. For only mutual funds, you don't need a Demat account.
✓
Start your first SIP — even ₹500/month
Pick a Nifty 50 index fund (UTI Nifty 50 Index, HDFC Index Fund, or ICICI Pru Nifty 50). Set up a monthly SIP. Starting small and staying consistent beats waiting until you "have enough."
